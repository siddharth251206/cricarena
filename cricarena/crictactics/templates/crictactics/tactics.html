Batting Shots

  • Bye Area: When batter misses the ball and runs are scored as byes.
  • Late Cut: Finessed cut played late toward third man.
  • Leg Glance: Redirecting a leg-side ball with wrists to fine leg.
  • Hook: Short ball pulled in the air to leg side, often square leg.
  • Pull: Horizontal shot to short deliveries, directed to mid-wicket.
  • Big Shot: Aerial shot played to score a boundary or six.
  • On Drive: Elegant ground shot toward mid-on.
  • Straight Drive: Driven past the bowler down the ground.
  • Off Drive: Classic drive through extra cover region.
  • Cover Drive: Stylish shot through the covers region.
  • Square Cut: Square shot to short, wide deliveries outside off.

Fielding Positions

  • Pitch center: Batter (striker), non-striker, wicket-keeper (WK), bowler, and umpires.
Field division:
  • Off side (left of batter from their perspective) ‚Äì positions like Point, Cover, and Third Man.
  • Leg side/On side (right of batter) ‚Äì positions like Square Leg, Mid-wicket, and Fine Leg.
Fielding Positions
  • Close-in positions (near the batter):
    • Slips, Gully, Silly Point, Silly Mid-off/on, Short Leg, Leg Gully.
  • Inner ring (inside the 30-yard circle):
    • Point, Cover, Extra Cover, Mid-off, Mid-on, Mid-wicket, Square Leg.
  • Deep/outfield positions (near boundary):
    • Third Man, Deep Point, Deep Cover, Long-off, Long-on, Deep Mid-wicket, Deep Square Leg, Fine Leg, Long Leg.
  • Special: Long Stop (directly behind WK, rarely used).
Glossary
  • Short: Nearer to batter.
  • Silly: Very close to batter (dangerous positions).
  • Deep: Farther from batter, near the boundary.
  • Wide: Farther from the pitch's line.
  • Fine vs Straight vs Square:
    • Fine: Near line of pitch.
    • Straight: Along batter‚Äôs straight hit.
    • Square: Perpendicular to batter.
Other Elements
  • Mandatory fielders: Bowler and wicket-keeper.
  • 30-yard circle: Marks inner fielding restriction area.
  • Umpires: Main umpire (U) and square leg umpire (Sq L U).
  • Batsmen: Striker (S), non-striker (NS), runner (R).

Swing Bowling Techniques

  • Normal Swing:
    • Grip: Seam angled with shiny side facing slip or leg.
    • Mechanism: Ball swings in direction of seam due to pressure difference.
    • Optimum speed: ~70 mph
  • Reverse Swing:
    • Grip: Same as normal, but roughness on both sides aids swing in reverse.
    • Mechanism: Ball swings opposite to seam direction.
    • Optimum speed: ~90 mph
  • Contrast Swing:
    • At low speed: Ball swings toward smooth side.
    • At high speed: Ball swings toward shiny side (if seam is upright).
    • Optimum speed: 70‚Äì80 mph depending on seam and speed.

Rules of Cricket

  1. The Basics
    • Objective: Two teams of 11 players each take turns batting and fielding.
    • Goal: Score more runs than the opponent.
    • Match formats: Test matches, One-Day Internationals (ODIs), and T20s, differing mainly in overs and match duration.
  2. The Playing Area
    • Pitch: 22 yards long, where bowling and batting happen.
    • Field: Circular/oval with a boundary marking.
    • Creases:
      • Bowling crease ‚Äì where the bowler delivers the ball.
      • Popping crease ‚Äì where the batter must be to be ‚Äúsafe‚Äù from a run-out.
      • Return creases ‚Äì keep the bowler within legal limits.
  3. Innings and Overs
    • Innings: One team bats while the other fields.
    • Over: 6 legal balls bowled by one bowler.
    • Bowlers can‚Äôt bowl consecutive overs.
  4. Batting Rules
    • Batters score runs by:
      • Running between the wickets.
      • Hitting boundaries:
        • 4 runs if the ball crosses the boundary after bouncing.
        • 6 runs if it clears the boundary without bouncing.
    • Batters can be dismissed by:
      • Bowled ‚Äì Ball hits the stumps.
      • Caught ‚Äì Ball caught by fielder before touching the ground.
      • LBW (Leg Before Wicket) ‚Äì Ball would hit stumps but strikes batter‚Äôs leg/pad.
      • Run out ‚Äì Batter outside crease when stumps are broken.
      • Stumped ‚Äì Wicket-keeper breaks stumps when batter is out of crease.
      • Hit wicket, Handled the ball, Obstructing the field, Timed out, Hit the ball twice.
  5. Bowling Rules
    • Must bowl with a straight arm; throwing is illegal.
    • Must stay behind the bowling crease and within return creases.
    • No-ball: Overstepping, dangerous deliveries, or illegal action ‚Üí 1 run to batting side and free hit in limited-overs cricket.
    • Wide ball: Too far from batter to hit ‚Üí 1 run to batting side.
  6. Fielding Rules
    • Only 11 players on field, including a wicket-keeper.
    • Fielding restrictions:
      • In limited-overs: Powerplay overs require a certain number of fielders inside the 30-yard circle.
    • Catches must be taken cleanly (ball must not touch ground).
  7. Scoring Runs
    • Batters run between the two sets of stumps after hitting the ball.
    • Runs completed when both batters cross and reach the opposite crease.
    • Extras:
      • Byes ‚Äì runs from balls missed by the batter and wicket-keeper.
      • Leg byes ‚Äì runs from ball hitting batter‚Äôs body (not bat).
      • No-balls and Wides add runs without counting in batter‚Äôs statistics.
  8. Match Outcomes
    • Win: Team with most runs after all innings wins.
    • Tie: Both teams score the same runs (rare).
    • Draw: In Test cricket, if time runs out before the match finishes.
    • Super Over: Used to break ties in limited-overs matches.
  9. Umpires and Decisions
    • On-field umpires oversee play, signal decisions (out, no-ball, wide, boundary, etc.).
    • Third umpire (with video review) for TV replays and close calls.
    • DRS (Decision Review System): Teams can challenge certain umpire decisions.
  10. Spirit of the Game
    • Players must act within the ‚ÄúSpirit of Cricket‚Äù ‚Äì respect opponents, officials, and rules.
    • Unsportsmanlike behavior (sledging, tampering, etc.) can lead to penalties.
